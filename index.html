Abstract

I am an M.A. candidate in Quantitative Methods in the Social Sciences at Columbia University, working on market microstructure, stochastic finance, financial econometrics, and market design. My current research studies Automated Market Makers, CEX--DEX arbitrage, liquidity provision, inventory risk, and execution quality using stochastic modeling, simulation, and empirical market analysis.

Prior to Columbia, I completed a B.Sc. in Mathematics with a minor in Economics at Lady Shri Ram College for Women, University of Delhi. My research background spans DeFi market microstructure, causal inference, transport economics, urban policy, fiscal ranking methodologies, and quantitative public-policy analysis.

Personal Projects

Deep Reinforcement Learning & Systematic Trading Strategies

Extended Sood et al. (2023) into a PPO portfolio-allocation framework integrating earnings-transcript sentiment, macro-regime, and implied-volatility features across equities, fixed income, commodities, and cash; stress-tested over the 2020 COVID shock and 2022 tightening cycle.

Portfolio Optimization via Mixed-Integer Quadratic Programming

Formulated portfolio construction as a Mixed-Integer Quadratic Program in Gurobi with cardinality, minimum-lot, and sector-concentration constraints; benchmarked against mean-variance and equal-weight portfolios.

Systematic Trading Dashboard & Backtesting Framework

Built an IMC Prosperity trading dashboard and Python backtester using SMA, EMA, RSI, Bollinger Bands, volatility filters, signal visualization, parameter sweeps, transaction-cost assumptions, and performance attribution; ranked 85th nationally in the United States in the 2026 Prosperity Challenge.

MPATH-India
