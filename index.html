Abstract

I am an M.A. candidate in Quantitative Methods in the Social Sciences at Columbia University, working on market microstructure, stochastic finance, financial econometrics, and market design. My current research studies Automated Market Makers, CEX–DEX arbitrage, liquidity provision, inventory risk, and execution quality using stochastic modeling, simulation, and empirical market analysis. I am particularly interested in what happens to the classical microstructure objects — routing, price discovery, latency, and adverse selection — when liquidity for the same asset is fragmented across venues with very different settlement and information properties.

Prior to Columbia, I completed a B.Sc. in Mathematics (Honors) with a minor in Economics at Lady Shri Ram College for Women, University of Delhi. Along the way I have worked on fiscal ranking methodologies and trade analysis at NITI Aayog, India's federal policy think tank; on transport economics and commuting behaviour at SPIESR, work that grew into two Springer chapters; and on causal program evaluation at Columbia's Institute for Social and Economic Research and Policy. My research background accordingly spans DeFi market microstructure, causal inference, transport economics, urban policy, and quantitative public-policy analysis.

About

My research interests are Market Microstructure, Quantitative Finance, Financial Econometrics, Causal Inference, Stochastic Optimization, and Algorithmic Trading. I am particularly interested in how market design, latency, liquidity concentration, and routing incentives shape outcomes in decentralized and centralized markets: where price discovery actually happens when the same asset trades on many venues at once, how arbitrageurs and liquidity providers respond to the frictions each venue imposes, and what this implies for the design of exchanges and lending markets.

Methodologically, I work with stochastic processes and simulation, econometric and causal-inference designs (difference-in-differences, panel methods, quasi-experimental estimators), and optimization — from mixed-integer programming to reinforcement learning. I care about empirical work that is reproducible end to end: my projects ship with their data-collection pipelines and code.

Research

Market Design and Liquidity Provision in Automated Market Makers

Work in Progress · Mentored research under Prof. Steven Campbell, Columbia University

Developing stochastic models of liquidity provision, inventory risk, and cross-exchange arbitrage across centralized and decentralized exchanges. The project asks how order routing, price discovery, and latency behave when liquidity for the same asset is fragmented across multiple venues; how arbitrage links centralized and decentralized markets and how quickly mispricings close; and how adverse selection — informed flow picking off passive liquidity providers — manifests differently across venue designs and feeds back into routing incentives. The approach combines stochastic modeling and simulation with empirical analysis of cross-venue trade and quote data.

Commute Distance and Residential Characteristics: Weathering the Storm

Lecture Notes in Civil Engineering, Springer Nature Singapore. Published. Contributor.

Tests whether the developed-country finding that residential self-selection plays little role in commute distance holds in India, using the nationally representative 76th round of the NSSO. Over two-thirds of job-related trips cover under 5 km, relocation for employment is rare, and household location and physical characteristics show negligible-to-weak association with commute distance — implying that the four-step transport model cannot be the sole basis for transport planning in Indian cities. Presented at IMRC 2024, IIM Ahmedabad, and TPMDC 2024, IIT Bombay. Link

Limits to Commute: The Case of Indian Women

Lecture Notes in Civil Engineering, Springer Nature Singapore, 2024. Published. Contributor.

Naga Siva Gayatri Dittakavi and Nachiket Gosavi

Examines whether the gender gap in commuting documented in developed economies appears in India, drawing on household-level data from the 76th NSSO round. Stratified regressions compare women's commute patterns across urban and rural settings and identify their determinants, with implications for women-centric transport policy. Link

Personal Projects

Deep Reinforcement Learning & Systematic Trading Strategies

Extended Sood et al. (2023) into a PPO portfolio-allocation framework integrating earnings-transcript sentiment, macro-regime indicators, and options-implied volatility features across equities, fixed income, commodities, and cash, with turnover penalties and ESG exclusions built into the action space. Applied SHAP for explainability of the learned policy and stress-tested the agent over the 2020 COVID shock and the 2022 tightening cycle, where it outperformed constrained mean-variance optimization on Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar, and turnover-adjusted net returns.

Portfolio Optimization via Mixed-Integer Quadratic Programming

Formulated portfolio construction as a Mixed-Integer Quadratic Program in Gurobi, encoding the constraints institutional portfolios actually face — cardinality limits, minimum-lot thresholds, and sector-concentration caps — within a mean-variance objective. Benchmarked the resulting portfolios against unconstrained mean-variance and equal-weight (1/N) baselines across equity, fixed-income, and commodity universes, quantifying the efficiency cost of each constraint class and its sensitivity along the risk-return frontier.

Systematic Trading Dashboard & Backtesting Framework
